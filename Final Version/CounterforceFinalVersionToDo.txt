	-Satellite launch facilities can either be built or captured.
	-Satellites cost money and 1 millon electricity resources.
	-Satellites come in two orbit types: Geosynchronous (they stay in one location on the map) or whatever the classical orbit type is, where they pass in a ring around the planet.
		-Both orbit types can be customized: players choose the location for a geosync orbit, and they choose two points that make an orbit for the non-geosync types. 
		-Non-geosync orbits can be altered at the cost of electricity.
	-Satellites come in two versions: Defense and recon.
		-Recon uses electricity to conduct radar scans. 
		-Defense uses electricity (probably 200,000 kw per shot) to use a laser to destroy ICBMs and MIRVs. 
	-When a satellite runs out of electricity, it "de-orbits" and is destroyed. 
	-ASAT interceptors and high-altitude EMP are the only ways to destroy satellites.

-Rail Yards and trains.
	-Trains use fuel, coal, or electricity to transfer resources in great quantities. 
	-Rail yards must be linked to other ones. Only friendly yards can be linked. The yards must be within 50-100 km of eachother to link together. 
	-Fuel trains have the most capacity.
	-Coal trains are the cheapest to operate but have short range and half the capacity of the fuel train.
	-Electric trains are fast and efficient, but have only 1/4 the capacity of the fuel train.
	-Why is this useful when warehouses already function as such terminals? Because the 100 km link range of the depot allows players to link without having to travel that distance.
		-IDEA: Rail Terminals must be linked to eachother manually. Each link costs a certain amount of lumber and iron to purchase. The longer the line, the more espensive it is.
